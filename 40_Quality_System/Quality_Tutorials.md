This standard defines the minimum quality expectations for Tutorial Prep and tutorial outputs.

## Minimum requirements

Every serious tutorial should include:
1. **Clear objective** — what the learner will understand, build, or achieve
2. **Prerequisites** — what must already be known, available, or installed
3. **Setup instructions** — environment or tooling setup where needed
4. **Verification steps** — how the learner can confirm success

## Good quality signals

Strong tutorials usually also include:
- a clear scope
- a visible target audience
- explanation before complexity spikes
- concrete checkpoints instead of vague encouragement
- troubleshooting or failure-handling where relevant

## Anti-patterns

Avoid tutorials that:
- assume too much without stating prerequisites
- hide success criteria
- skip setup even when setup matters
- read like unstructured notes rather than guided learning
- dump code or steps without framing what the learner is trying to achieve
